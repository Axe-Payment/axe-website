Products

Six modules. One contract. One integration.

Banks turn modules on as they need them. Most start with on-ramp and add transfers in month two.

On-ramp · Buy

Convert PKR to crypto in seconds — without leaving your app.

Bank customer initiates a buy in your app. axe. quotes across every connected VASP, picks the best execution, and settles to the customer's wallet. PKR is debited via RAAST or IBFT. The customer sees your brand the whole time.

  • Sub-second quotes from a multi-VASP order book.
  • RAAST · 1LINK · card · mobile wallet PSPs supported.
  • Webhook on settled — typical 4–8 seconds end-to-end.
Read the on-ramp spec
Off-ramp · Sell

Turn crypto into PKR, settle to a bank account the same day.

Customer sells held crypto from inside your app. axe. nets the trade across VASPs, hedges the exposure, and pushes PKR back to the customer's account through RAAST or IBFT. T+0 for retail, T+1 for institutional volumes.

  • Net-zero principal at axe. — every leg attributed to one Order.
  • FX exposure neutralised at quote time — no slippage to the bank.
  • SBP-format reconciliation file delivered daily by 06:00 PKT.

Transfer · Cross-border

Stablecoin transfers with FATF-grade originator data.

Move USDT, USDC, or PYUSD between wallets — including between VASPs in different jurisdictions. Travel Rule payload assembled and exchanged automatically using IVMS 101. Failed counterparty exchanges are blocked at submission, not after settlement.

  • Travel Rule via TRP, TRUST, Notabene, or Sumsub — pick one or all.
  • Per-corridor threshold and policy controls.
  • Counterparty due-diligence registry maintained by axe.
Read the transfer spec
Smart Routing

One API, optimal execution across every VASP and PSP.

For each order axe. scores every counterparty on cost, depth, latency, and live uptime — then routes the leg to whichever path wins. When a VASP degrades, fail-over is automatic. The bank sees one consistent SLA.

  • Per-tenant routing weights — exclude or prefer specific counterparties.
  • Live health probes; degraded counterparties cool off automatically.
  • Decision log on every order — auditable down to the millisecond.

FAQ

The questions every bank brings to the first meeting.

If yours is not listed, we'll send the spec and a regulator letter on request.

Is axe. a VASP?
No. axe. is an orchestration layer that routes between licensed VASPs and licensed PSPs. We never hold customer crypto and never carry a principal balance in fiat. Custody and licensing live with our partners — provable from our network and ledger data, and a feature our customers' regulators specifically prefer.
Does the bank need a new licence to use axe.?
No. The bank operates under its existing licence as a commercial bank, EMI, or PSO. axe. partners hold the VASP and PSP licences. We provide the regulator-facing documentation that explains this and join calls with SBP and PVARA when required.
Which assets are supported at launch?
Major stablecoins (USDT, USDC, PYUSD), BTC, and ETH at launch. Long-tail tokens are added per bank request, subject to per-corridor risk and Travel Rule readiness.
What does the customer journey look like?
The customer never leaves the bank's app. axe. is invisible infrastructure — the bank renders its own UI, calls our API, and receives signed webhooks. The customer sees a typical 4–8 second end-to-end on-ramp.
How long is integration?
Eight to twelve weeks from signed contract to live API in production for a bank with a published developer portal. Sandbox keys are issued on day one. The longest pole is typically the bank's internal compliance review, not the engineering work.
What happens during a VASP outage?
Smart routing detects degraded counterparties via continuous health probes and shifts new orders to other VASPs. In-flight orders complete on the original counterparty unless they are clearly stuck — in which case our admin runbook initiates manual rerouting. The bank sees one consistent SLA.
What does pricing look like?
A blended bps fee per transaction (30–80 bps depending on volume and corridor) plus a base platform fee that funds dedicated regulatory and engineering capacity. We avoid the bps-only model — it commoditises the relationship and starves the work that actually matters for a regulated launch.
